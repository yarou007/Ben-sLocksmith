We show up with the right parts when possible, explain repair vs replacement options, and focus on durable fixes that reduce call-backs. If compliance documentation is needed for your opening, we can help you understand what’s required and what’s actually fixable on-site.

Recent Work in Washington DC

Below are a few representative examples of the kinds of commercial door and locksmith calls we handle in Washington DC. Details vary by opening and hardware, but these illustrate our approach: diagnose quickly, repair safely, and leave you with a door that closes, latches, and secures correctly.

Retail Store: Door not latching during busy hours

  • Issue: latch would catch intermittently; staff had to “pull the door” to lock it
  • Root cause: strike alignment + door sag/hinge wear
  • Fix: realigned strike and hardware, adjusted closer so the door closes with consistent latch engagement
  • Outcome: secure latch every close; fewer lockouts and no “propped” door workarounds

Related services: Door Hardware · Commercial Locksmith

Restaurant: Panic bar dragging / hard to open

  • Issue: panic bar required excess force; door rubbed and didn’t always re-latch
  • Root cause: device/door alignment + closer settings causing inconsistent closing speed
  • Fix: adjusted exit device and door hardware, tuned closer sweep/latch to restore proper closing and operation
  • Outcome: smoother egress, reliable relatch, reduced wear on the device

Related services: Panic Bar Repair · Exit Devices · Door Closers

Office Suite: Rekey and key control after staff turnover

  • Issue: uncontrolled key copies and unknown key holders
  • Plan: rekey critical doors first, then align remaining cylinders to a controlled keying plan
  • Fix: rekeyed cylinders and issued a documented key list to support ongoing key control
  • Outcome: restored access control and reduced security risk with minimal downtime

Related services: Commercial Locksmith · Master Key Systems

After-hours emergency: Storefront secured after a break-in

  • Issue: compromised lock/door hardware and immediate need to secure the opening
  • Fix: temporary securing as needed, hardware replacement/rekey, and recommendations for follow-up hardening
  • Outcome: business secured the same night and ready for next-day operations
