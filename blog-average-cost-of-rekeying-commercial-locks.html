What Is the Average Cost of Re-Keying Commercial Locks in DC?

Commercial rekeying is often the fastest way to regain key control after employee turnover, lost keys, or tenant changes. Here is how pricing typically works.

Typical Price Ranges (Commercial)

  • Single cylinder rekey: generally lower cost entry point
  • Multi-door office rekey: moderate per-door cost with volume efficiency
  • Master key rekey projects: higher due to hierarchy planning and pinning complexity

Final pricing depends on lock brand, number of cylinders, service urgency, and whether new keys or restricted keyways are required.

Main Cost Drivers

  • High-security cylinders (Medeco, BEST, etc.)
  • After-hours emergency dispatch
  • Large portfolio or multi-suite projects
  • Need for master key redesign

Rekey vs Full Replacement

Rekey is usually more cost-effective when hardware is in good condition. Full replacement is better for damaged locks, outdated hardware, or major security upgrades.

How to Keep Costs Predictable

  • Keep an updated key inventory
  • Standardize lock platforms where possible
  • Use planned rekey windows instead of emergency calls
