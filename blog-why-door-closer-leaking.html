Commercial Door Closer Guide

Why Is My Commercial Door Closer Leaking?

Oil leakage usually means the closer has lost hydraulic pressure. Once that happens, door speed control becomes unreliable and the opening can fail to latch safely.

Most Common Causes

  • Worn seals from high opening/closing cycles
  • Body damage from door abuse or forced operation
  • Incorrect closer size for door weight and traffic
  • Aging hardware beyond service life

What Happens If You Ignore It

  • Door slams, drifts, or fails to latch
  • Increased wear on hinges, locks, and frame
  • Life-safety and compliance risk on fire-rated openings
  • Security gaps when doors do not close fully

Can a Leaking Closer Be Repaired?

In most commercial cases, replacement is more reliable than attempting an internal seal repair. A new closer properly sized and adjusted usually restores safe operation the scheduled.

When to Call Emergency Service

  • Main entrance slamming or not closing
  • Fire door not self-closing
  • Tenant or customer safety complaints
