What Happens If a Fire Door Fails Inspection?

A failed inspection usually means you need documented corrections before the opening is considered compliant again. In active-use buildings, delays can increase risk and disrupt operations.

Call 703-244-0559Deficiency Repair Service

Common Consequences

  • Corrective actions required before close-out
  • Operational pressure for high-traffic doors
  • Increased liability if life-safety doors stay deficient
  • Scheduling strain for property and maintenance teams
  • Potential follow-up review timelines

The faster you map and complete corrections, the faster risk decreases.

Immediate Next Steps

  • Prioritize doors with active egress/latching failure
  • Bundle corrections by building zone
  • Document every corrected item clearly
  • Prepare for follow-up verification
