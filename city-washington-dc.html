Commercial Glass Repair in Washington DC — Code Compliant & Fast

Washington DC has some of the most demanding commercial property standards in the country. DCRA enforces IBC requirements aggressively — and historic preservation districts like Georgetown and Dupont Circle add additional review requirements for exterior commercial modifications including storefront glass.

We've completed commercial glass projects for restaurants on 14th Street, retail storefronts on M Street in Georgetown, office buildings on K Street, and government-adjacent commercial properties throughout the District. Our technicians are DC DCRA-licensed and familiar with historic district requirements.

DC-Specific Commercial Glass Requirements

  • All storefront glass in DC must meet IBC Chapter 24 glazing requirements
  • Historic preservation areas (Georgetown, Capitol Hill, etc.) may require DCRA SHPO review for storefront system changes
  • DC Green Building Act compliance — low-e glass required on certain commercial projects
  • All glazing contractors in DC must hold a valid DCRA contractor license

Common DC Commercial Glass Emergencies

  • Break-in board-up — Georgetown retail, H Street NE, Navy Yard
  • Restaurant exterior glass — patio dividers, entry doors, window replacement
  • Office building glass — curtain wall, lobby glass, conference room partitions
  • Government-adjacent commercial — we understand proximity security requirements
