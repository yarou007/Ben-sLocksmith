Commercial Locksmith & Exit Door Hardware in Washington DC

Washington DC's commercial buildings face some of the country's most rigorous code enforcement. DCRA inspects commercial exit hardware aggressively, and non-compliant panic bars, closers, or fire door assemblies can result in failed occupancy inspections and stop-work orders. Our DC DCRA-licensed technicians understand these requirements and document every installation to code.

DC-Specific Requirements We Know

  • DC Building Code (IBC 2018 with local amendments) — Chapter 10 egress requirements
  • DCRA commercial contractor licensing — we hold active DC licenses
  • Historic district hardware — Georgetown, Capitol Hill, Dupont Circle preservation considerations
  • ADA Title III accessible hardware compliance — lever handles, low-force closers
