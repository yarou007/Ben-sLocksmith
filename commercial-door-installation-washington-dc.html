A measured site assessment helps prevent incompatible parts and change orders.

Diagnosis and scope

Opening types to discuss

The site condition and project documents determine what can be quoted. The request should identify the wall/frame condition and whether existing hardware is to be reused, replaced or coordinated with another trade.

Key points

  • Hollow metal doors and frames
  • Interior commercial wood doors where applicable
  • Storefront aluminum door applications
  • Fire-rated door and frame assemblies
  • Paired doors and exit openings
  • Openings receiving access-control hardware

Commercial decision support

What to include in an estimate request

Photos are useful for the first conversation, but final selection usually needs field dimensions and verification of the existing opening. If a general contractor or architect controls the project, attach the relevant door schedule and hardware set.

Key points

  • Opening location, quantity and approximate size
  • Existing door/frame material and visible damage
  • Handing, swing and traffic/egress function
  • Required rating or labels shown in project documents
  • Lock, closer, panic/exit and access-control needs
  • Desired schedule, site hours and delivery constraints

Service details

Repair or replace?

A repair may be the better commercial decision when the door and frame remain sound and a correctable hardware or alignment issue causes the failure. Replacement becomes more reasonable when the opening is damaged, repeatedly fails, is incompatible with required hardware or cannot support the intended use.

The estimate should separate immediate security work from the planned replacement if the existing opening must remain usable while materials are coordinated.

Key points

  • Repair: localized, serviceable problem with a sound opening
  • Replace: extensive damage, incompatibility or repeated failure
  • Phase: temporary security followed by measured installation
  • Coordinate: clarify responsibilities across door, electrical and finish trades

Commercial FAQ

Questions about this commercial service

Can I get a commercial door estimate from photos?

Photos can start the scope, but final pricing or ordering may require field measurement, handing, frame/wall review and hardware verification.

Do you install hollow metal doors and frames?

Hollow metal openings are within the planning scope described here. Final availability, fire rating, preparation and related trade work must be confirmed in the estimate.

Can access control be coordinated with a new door?

Yes, the door-side preparation can be discussed with the access-control requirements. The scope must identify who provides power, controls, cabling, devices and commissioning.

When is repair better than replacement?

Repair is often preferable when the door/frame is sound and the failure is limited to serviceable hardware or alignment. A site assessment helps compare the two options.
