Start with the problem

Repair the failed lock without overlooking the door

Commercial lock failures can start inside the mortise case, cylinder, lever assembly or latch. They can also be caused by a door that has shifted and places pressure on otherwise working hardware.

A useful diagnosis compares lock operation with the door open and closed. That distinction helps show whether the repair belongs at the lock, strike, hinges, closer or frame.

!A mortise lock case is jammed or will not retract
!The cylinder spins, pulls loose or will not accept the key
!A lever handle droops or moves without retracting the latch
!The latch bolt sticks inside the lock body
!The key is difficult to turn even when the door is open
!The lock works open but binds when the door reaches the frame
!A deadbolt will not project into the strike
!The lock area was damaged by impact or forced entry

Diagnosis and scope

Mortise lock and lever failures

Mortise locks combine several functions inside the door edge. A worn hub, spring, latch or internal connection can create loose handles, incomplete retraction or a lock that jams under use.

Key points

  • Loose or drooping commercial levers
  • Latch that will not retract fully
  • Key operation that no longer controls the lock
  • Internal binding or intermittent operation

Commercial decision support

Cylinder, key and deadbolt problems

A failed cylinder may turn without operating the lock, bind around a damaged key or loosen from the door. Cylinder work should preserve the intended key operation and fit the existing commercial hardware.

Key points

  • Loose, spinning or seized cylinders
  • Broken key extraction and keyway inspection
  • Deadbolt projection and strike engagement
  • Cylinder repair, rekey or replacement assessment

Service details

When alignment looks like lock failure

If a lock operates normally while the door is open but binds at the frame, the strike position, door sag or closer pressure may be the real cause. Replacing the lock alone would leave that condition in place.

Key points

  • Open-door versus closed-door function test
  • Latch and strike contact inspection
  • Hinge, pivot and frame movement checks
  • Coordination with closer or full door repair

Commercial FAQ

Questions about this commercial service

Can a commercial mortise lock be repaired instead of replaced?

It depends on the failed component, wear and compatibility of the existing lock. Diagnosis should separate a repairable internal problem from damage that makes replacement more practical.

Why does the key turn when the door is open but bind when it is closed?

The latch or deadbolt may be pressing against the strike because the door or frame has shifted. The alignment should be corrected rather than assuming the cylinder is the only problem.

Does a spinning cylinder always require a new lock?

No. The cylinder, retaining hardware and lock interface need inspection. The appropriate fix may involve securing or replacing the cylinder rather than replacing the full lock.

What information helps identify commercial lock hardware?

Share a clear photo of the door edge, lever or pull, cylinder and any visible markings through an approved channel. Door material and the way the lock behaves open and closed also help define the repair scope.
