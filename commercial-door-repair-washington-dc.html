Start with the problem

Secure the opening before the failure spreads

A commercial door that will not close, latch or lock can interrupt operations and leave the property exposed. The failure may involve the door, frame, hinges, closer, latch, lock or egress hardware rather than a single replaceable part.

Emergency door repair starts by identifying what changed at the opening and whether the door can still move safely. Repair decisions should restore controlled movement and secure latching without hiding a frame or alignment problem.

!The commercial door will not close into the frame
!The latch will not engage and the opening cannot be secured
!A storefront entry drags, binds or stays partly open
!Exit hardware will not release or relatch correctly
!The closer is leaking, disconnected or unable to control the door
!Hinges, pivots or the frame are visibly misaligned
!The door or frame was damaged during a break-in
!A lock or latch failed while the business was operating
!A fire-rated opening will not self-close and latch

Diagnosis and scope

Doors that will not close or latch

A door can miss the strike because the frame shifted, hinges wore, the closer lost control or the latch no longer projects correctly. Adjusting only one component may not solve a problem involving the full opening.

Key points

  • Door and frame alignment diagnosis
  • Hinge, pivot and fastener problems
  • Latch and strike engagement
  • Closer control and final latching action
  • Threshold or floor interference

Commercial decision support

Storefront and secured-entry failures

High-use storefront and business entries can become difficult to secure after repeated traffic, impact or component wear. The lock may be functional while the door position prevents it from engaging.

Key points

  • Aluminum storefront door alignment
  • Lock, latch and strike coordination
  • Loose pull, lever and mounting hardware
  • Damaged pivots, sweeps and thresholds
  • Temporary stabilization planning when parts are needed

Service details

Break-in, egress and life-safety door problems

Forced entry can distort the frame, tear fasteners or damage the lock area beyond what is visible. Exit doors also need hardware that releases from the egress side and allows the door to close and latch afterward.

Key points

  • Door, frame and lock-area damage assessment
  • Failed panic or exit hardware diagnosis
  • Closer and latching problems on fire-rated openings
  • Repair-versus-replacement scope for damaged assemblies

Commercial FAQ

Questions about this commercial service

Why does a commercial door close but not latch?

The latch may miss the strike because of hinge wear, frame movement, closer settings, door sag or a damaged latch. The opening should be checked as a system before replacing the lock.

Can a storefront door be repaired if it drags and will not lock?

Often the door, pivots, frame, threshold, lock and strike must be evaluated together. The appropriate repair depends on which components are worn or out of alignment.

What should a business report after break-in damage?

Describe whether the opening can close and secure, where the impact occurred, and whether the frame or lock area is distorted. Photos can help define the initial scope through the approved request form.

How is an urgent commercial door repair scoped?

The first priority is identifying whether the opening moves safely and can secure. Immediate stabilization can be separated from permanent repair or replacement when damaged parts or a larger door-and-frame scope are involved.
