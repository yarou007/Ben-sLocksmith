Start with the problem

Commercial lock problems need a business-focused diagnosis

A failed lock can interrupt staff access, leave a tenant suite unsecured or expose gaps in key control. The right next step depends on the opening, the hardware condition and who should retain access.

Commercial locksmith work can include entry, repair, cylinder changes, rekeying and access planning. The goal is to solve the immediate lock problem while avoiding unnecessary changes to working door hardware.

!Staff cannot enter a locked office, suite or service area
!A key turns but the commercial lock does not release
!Keys are missing after a staffing or vendor change
!A storefront or office lock no longer secures the opening
!A mortise lock, lever or cylinder feels loose or jammed
!The current master-key structure no longer matches access roles
!A broken key is lodged in a commercial cylinder
!Forced entry has compromised the lock or key-control plan

Diagnosis and scope

Commercial lockouts and failed-lock help

Office and business lockouts are not all key problems. A binding latch, shifted door, damaged cylinder or failed mortise case can keep the opening locked even when the correct key is present.

Key points

  • Office, retail and managed-suite lockouts
  • Broken-key and non-turning cylinder diagnosis
  • Mortise, lever and deadbolt function checks
  • Door and strike alignment checks before lock replacement

Commercial decision support

Rekeys, cylinders and key-control changes

When hardware remains serviceable, changing the key operation may be more practical than replacing the entire lock. Rekey scope should reflect employee access, tenant turnover and the number of affected openings.

Key points

  • Employee or contractor access changes
  • Lost-key and unknown-copy concerns
  • Commercial cylinder replacement when rekeying is not suitable
  • Master-key hierarchy review for controlled access

Service details

Access planning for managed properties

Property and facility teams often need a repeatable way to identify doors, key groups and authorized users. Clear scope reduces accidental cross-access between suites and helps future changes stay manageable.

Key points

  • Door and suite inventory planning
  • Key-holder and access-level review
  • Phased rekey planning for occupied properties
  • Coordination with door repair and access-control needs

Commercial FAQ

Questions about this commercial service

Can you help when the correct key will not open a business door?

Yes. The cause may be inside the lock, at the latch and strike, or in the door alignment. Diagnosing the full opening helps identify whether repair, adjustment or a cylinder change is appropriate.

Should a business rekey or replace its locks?

Rekeying may be suitable when the hardware works and only key access must change. Replacement may be appropriate when the lock is damaged, unreliable or unsuitable for the opening.

Can commercial locksmith work include several tenant suites?

A multi-opening scope can be planned by suite, door and access group. Property authorization and the intended key hierarchy should be clear before work begins.

What details help identify the right commercial locksmith service?

Share the door location, property type, lock or cylinder style, current key behavior and whether the opening can close and secure. Those details help separate lock, alignment and access-control needs.
