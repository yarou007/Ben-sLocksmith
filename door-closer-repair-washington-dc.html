Start with the problem

Closer behavior can reveal problems elsewhere in the opening

A closer controls motion, but it cannot reliably overcome a dragging door, worn hinge, shifted frame or misaligned latch. Diagnosis should separate closer faults from conditions that make the closer appear defective.

Commercial closer service considers the door, frame, hinges, latch and daily operating conditions so the repair addresses the full opening.

!Hydraulic fluid is visible on the closer or door
!Door slams before users can pass through

!Door reaches the frame but does not latch

!Door is difficult to open or stops abruptly

Diagnosis and scope

Adjustment versus replacement

Sweep, latch and backcheck behavior may be adjustable when the closer is intact and correctly sized for the opening. Visible leakage, damaged castings or loss of hydraulic control often point toward replacement rather than repeated adjustment.

Key points

  • Check for fluid loss and body damage
  • Inspect arm attachment and geometry
  • Observe sweep, latch and backcheck behavior
  • Compare door movement before changing settings

Commercial decision support

Why a door still will not latch

A closer may bring the door nearly shut while the latch misses the strike or the door binds at the frame. The repair plan should account for alignment and hardware resistance before increasing closing force.

Key points

  • Hinge, pivot or frame movement
  • Latch and strike misalignment
  • Threshold, weather seal or floor interference
  • Panic bar or exit-device resistance

Service details

Closer work on fire-rated openings

When a labeled fire door does not self-close and latch, the property team should identify the opening and share any inspection findings. The closer, latch, hinges and clearances must be considered together, and no repair should be represented as a guaranteed inspection result.

Key points

  • Record the opening label and supplied deficiency notes
  • Check closing from the fully open position
  • Observe latch engagement without manual assistance
  • Document additional door or hardware concerns

Commercial FAQ

Questions about this commercial service

Can a leaking commercial door closer be adjusted?

Adjustment does not restore lost hydraulic fluid or repair a damaged seal. A leaking closer should be assessed for replacement and for any door condition that contributed to the failure.

Why does the door closer shut the door but not latch it?

The latch phase may be too weak, but alignment, hinge wear, strike position, seals or exit hardware can also prevent latching. The whole opening should be checked.

Can slamming always be fixed with an adjustment?

No. Adjustment may help an intact closer, while internal failure, leakage, incorrect application or arm damage may require replacement or related door repair.

Can a commercial door closer be adjusted instead of replaced?

Adjustment may help when the closer is serviceable and the door, hinges and latch are aligned. Leaking fluid, damaged components or a closer that no longer controls the opening may require replacement assessment.
