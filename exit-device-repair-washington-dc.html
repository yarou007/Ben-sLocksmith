Start with the problem

Exit device repair starts with the configuration

Rim, mortise and vertical rod devices can produce similar symptoms for very different reasons. Identifying the configuration and testing every latch point prevents a one-size-fits-all repair.

Service covers commercial exit doors at businesses and managed properties, with diagnosis based on the installed device configuration and complete opening.

Diagnosis and scope

How exit-device configurations differ

A rim device latches at the surface of the frame, a mortise device operates a lock case within the door, and a rod device secures at the top, bottom or both. Diagnosis follows the actual latch path and the door preparation.

Key points

  • Rim latch and strike engagement
  • Mortise lock case and door-edge alignment
  • Surface vertical rod adjustment
  • Concealed rod movement and latch points

Commercial decision support

The device may not be the only fault

Door sag, warped frames, damaged pivots, closer pressure and meeting-stile alignment can prevent an otherwise functional device from releasing or relatching. Those conditions should be documented before parts are replaced.

Key points

  • Door and frame clearances
  • Hinge or pivot condition
  • Closer and coordinator behavior
  • Exterior trim and cylinder operation

Service details

Repair, component replacement or full replacement

Some faults can be corrected through alignment, secure attachment or serviceable component replacement. Extensive wear, damaged housings, unavailable components or unsuitable door preparation may support a complete replacement recommendation.

Key points

  • Identify repairable versus damaged components
  • Check mounting and door reinforcement
  • Review electrified functions before disconnecting hardware
  • Explain related door work separately from device work

What happens next

  2. Test every release and latch point

Commercial FAQ

Questions about this commercial service

What is the difference between a panic bar and an exit device?

Exit device is the broader hardware category. Panic hardware is a type of exit device intended for particular occupancy and egress applications. The existing opening should be identified before work is scoped.

Can vertical rod exit devices be repaired?

Many rod, latch and alignment problems can be serviced, but concealed components, door damage or unavailable parts may change the practical repair path.

Do you diagnose electrified exit-device problems?

The mechanical opening and visible electrified functions can be assessed. The final scope depends on the device, power path, access-control setup and any work requiring another qualified trade.

How can a business identify its exit-device configuration?

Photos of the push side, pull side, door edge, top and bottom latch points, and any visible model markings can help distinguish rim, mortise and vertical-rod hardware before the site assessment.
