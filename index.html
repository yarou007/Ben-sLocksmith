FAQ

Common Questions

What's the fastest you can respond to an emergency?
Our average on-site time is under 45 minutes anywhere in Washington DC, Northern Virginia, Maryland, and New York City. We dispatch immediately upon your call — live dispatcher, no voicemail, 24/7/365.
Is a broken panic bar really a code violation?
Yes — immediately. Panic bars are life-safety devices required by NFPA 101 (Life Safety Code) on all required exits in assembly occupancies with 50+ people. A non-functioning panic bar on a required exit is a fire code violation that can result in a failed inspection or occupancy revocation. Call us the moment you notice a problem.
Do you do commercial re-keys after employee termination?
Yes — this is one of our most common service calls. We re-key to a new master key system same day, provide a complete key inventory, and document everything for your HR records. We service Schlage, Medeco, Best, Corbin Russwin, and all major commercial lock brands.
What brands of door closers do you carry?
We stock LCN (4040 series), Norton, Dorma, and Sargent on our service trucks. These cover the vast majority of commercial door closer applications in the DC metro area and New York. For specialty or discontinued closers, we can typically source within 24 hours.
Are you licensed in all four states?
Yes. We hold commercial contractor licenses issued by DC DCRA, Virginia DPOR, Maryland DLLR, and New York City DOB. All technicians carry $2M general liability insurance and are background-checked. We can provide certificates of insurance and license numbers on request.

Why Commercial Locksmiths Are Different

Commercial locks operate under far greater stress than light-duty hardware. High-cycle mortise locks in a busy retail location may see 500+ operations per day. Master key systems require precise bitting charts and careful documentation. High-security cylinders like Medeco and Best require certified dealers and specialized tooling. Always hire a licensed commercial locksmith — not a low-experience provider calling themselves "commercial."
