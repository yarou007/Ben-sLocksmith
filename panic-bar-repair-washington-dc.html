Start with the problem

Repair the device and the opening as one system

A panic bar can appear to be the problem when the actual fault is door sag, strike alignment, closer behavior or binding elsewhere in the opening. A useful diagnosis checks the bar, latch, strike, hinges and closing action together.

Diagnosis and scope

Common panic bar repair paths

Repair may involve securing loose components, correcting alignment, restoring latch movement or replacing worn parts. The right path depends on the device condition and the way the door moves through the frame.

Key points

  • Touchpad, rail and end-cap inspection
  • Latch retraction and relatching checks
  • Strike and door-position correction
  • Dogging and exterior-trim diagnosis

Commercial decision support

When replacement may be the better option

Replacement may be more practical when the device body is damaged, critical parts are unavailable, the hardware has repeated failures or the existing configuration is unsuitable for the opening. The assessment should explain what can be repaired before replacement is proposed.

Key points

  • Extent of internal wear or impact damage
  • Condition of mounting points and door preparation
  • Parts availability for the existing device
  • Relationship to closer, lock and exterior trim

Service details

Fire-rated and egress openings need a complete review

If the panic bar is installed on a fire-rated or designated egress opening, the door, latch and closing hardware should be evaluated together. Project requirements depend on the opening and the applicable authority, so an assessment should not promise approval or inspection outcomes.

Key points

  • Confirm whether the opening is labeled or fire-rated
  • Note inspection findings supplied by the property team
  • Check that the door closes without binding
  • Document repair and replacement recommendations

Commercial FAQ

Questions about this commercial service

Can a stuck panic bar be repaired without replacing it?

Often, but the cause must be diagnosed first. Alignment, latch movement, loose components and door position can sometimes be corrected without replacing the complete device.

Why does the door open but fail to relatch?

Possible causes include a misaligned strike, door sag, closer problems, binding, worn latch parts or an obstruction within the device. The full opening should be checked.

Do you repair panic bars on fire-rated doors?

Commercial fire-rated openings can be assessed, but the door label, existing hardware and project requirements must be reviewed before a repair or replacement scope is recommended.

When should a commercial panic bar be repaired or replaced?

Repair may be practical when the device and door remain compatible and serviceable. Replacement may be appropriate when components are damaged, obsolete, incompatible with the opening or unable to deliver reliable release and relatching.
