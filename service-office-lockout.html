Start with the problem

Restore business access without guessing at the failure

An office lockout may be caused by a missing key, failed cylinder, jammed mortise lock, binding latch or a door that shifted against the strike. Describing the key and handle behavior helps distinguish access loss from hardware failure.

Authorization to enter the commercial space should be available before work begins. After entry, the lock and key-control condition can be reviewed if access may still be compromised.

Commercial decision support

Key problem or hardware failure?

If the expected key is present, its behavior can reveal whether the cylinder, internal lock, latch or door alignment is preventing entry. A credential issue may also be separate from the mechanical hardware.

Key points

  • Lost, broken or incorrect key
  • Cylinder that binds or spins
  • Lever or mortise mechanism that will not retract
  • Latch pressure caused by door alignment
  • Mechanical issue following credential acceptance

Commercial FAQ

Questions about this commercial service

What office lockout problems can be assessed?

Commercial office lockout service can assess failed keys, cylinders, mortise locks, levers and doors that will not release or open as expected.

Why will the correct office key turn without opening the door?

The cylinder may not be engaging the lock, the internal mechanism may have failed, or pressure at the latch may prevent retraction. The opening needs diagnosis before choosing a repair.

Should the office be rekeyed after a lost key?

Consider who may have the key, which doors it operates and whether copies can be accounted for. Rekeying can be scoped after access is restored if key control is uncertain.

Can an office lockout include follow-up lock service?

Yes. After access is restored, the commercial lock, cylinder and key-control condition can be assessed for repair or rekeying when needed.
