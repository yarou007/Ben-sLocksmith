Start with the problem

Storefront repair is more than a lock change

Aluminum storefront doors rely on narrow clearances and a connected set of pivots, closers, locks, latches and frame components. Treating only the most visible symptom can leave the door dragging, unsecured or difficult to use.

Service is designed for business and commercial entrances where the storefront door, frame, pivots, closer and locking hardware must operate together.

!Storefront door drags on the threshold or frame
!Door will not close fully or latch after entry
!Narrow-stile lock is difficult to turn or secure
!Concealed or surface closer no longer controls the door
!Top or bottom pivot feels loose or uneven
!Door leaf is visibly out of alignment in the frame
!Latch and strike no longer meet consistently
!Glass is loose or damaged and must be assessed before door work

Diagnosis and scope

Door, frame and pivot alignment

A storefront leaf can drop, twist or shift within the frame as pivots wear or mounting points move. Clearances should be observed at the head, jambs, meeting stile and threshold before lock or closer adjustments are made.

Key points

  • Top and bottom pivot condition
  • Frame and threshold clearances
  • Door sweep and weather-seal interference
  • Meeting-stile alignment on pairs of doors

Commercial decision support

Locks, latches and closer problems

A narrow-stile lock may bind because the door has moved, while a closer may fail to latch a door that drags. Diagnosis should distinguish a hardware fault from an alignment problem and explain any separate repair paths.

Key points

  • Narrow-stile lock and cylinder operation
  • Latch and strike engagement
  • Concealed or surface closer behavior
  • Pulls, handles and related attachment points

Service details

Glass conditions require scope confirmation

Glass condition can affect whether door and frame work can proceed safely, but glass replacement is not assumed in this service scope. Cracked, loose or displaced glazing should be assessed and may require a qualified glazing provider before or alongside door repair.

Key points

  • Photograph visible cracks or movement before service
  • Avoid operating a door with unstable glazing
  • Separate door-hardware work from glazing work
  • Confirm the responsible trade in the approved scope

Commercial FAQ

Questions about this commercial service

Why is my storefront door scraping the frame or threshold?

Worn pivots, loose mounting points, frame movement, door damage or threshold interference can shift the leaf. The opening should be assessed before closer or lock adjustments.

Can a storefront lock problem be caused by door alignment?

Yes. When the door drops or shifts, the latch, bolt and strike may stop lining up even if the lock itself still operates.

Does storefront door repair include broken glass replacement?

Not automatically. Glass condition is assessed because it affects safe door work, but replacement may require a qualified glazing provider and must be confirmed separately.

What details help scope a commercial storefront door repair?

Describe whether the door drags, closes, latches and locks, and share clear photos of the full opening, pivots or hinges, closer, lock and damaged areas through an approved channel.
